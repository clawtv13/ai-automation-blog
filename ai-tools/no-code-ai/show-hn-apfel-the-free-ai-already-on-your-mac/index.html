Show HN: Apfel – The free AI already on your Mac

HN Score: 132 points, 20 comments...

AC
Alex Chen
Builder & Automation Architect
📅 April 03, 2026 ⏱️ 4 min read

Your Mac already has powerful AI sitting unused. Most solopreneurs don't know it exists.

Meet Apfel - a free AI tool that runs directly on your Mac. No monthly fees. No data sharing with third parties.

Here's why this matters for your business and how to start using it today.

What Makes Apfel Different

Apfel runs locally on your computer. This means your business data stays private. No uploads to OpenAI or Google.

The tool uses your Mac's built-in Neural Engine. Every Mac from 2020 onward has this chip. You're already paying for the hardware.

Most AI tools cost $20-200 monthly. Apfel is completely free. No credit card required.

Real Business Applications

Sarah runs a marketing consultancy from Portland. She uses Apfel to draft client proposals. The tool saves her 3 hours weekly on initial drafts.

At her $150 hourly rate, that's $450 saved each week. Over a year, that's $23,400 in time value.

Tom manages an e-commerce store selling outdoor gear. He feeds customer reviews into Apfel for sentiment analysis. The insights help him spot product issues early.

Last month, Apfel flagged quality concerns in his hiking boots. Tom contacted the supplier before negative reviews spread. This saved him an estimated $8,000 in returns.

How to Set Up Apfel in 15 Minutes

The setup process is straightforward. No technical skills needed.

Step 1: Download and Install

Visit the Apfel website and download the Mac app. The file is about 2GB in size.

Drag the app to your Applications folder. Click to open it.

Step 2: First Run Setup

Apfel will download its AI model on first launch. This takes 10-15 minutes depending on your internet speed.

The app downloads once. After that, everything runs offline.

Step 3: Test Basic Functions

Start with simple tasks. Try drafting an email to a client. Or summarizing a long document.

The interface looks like a chat app. Type your request and hit enter.

Five Ways Solopreneurs Use Apfel

1. Content Creation

Draft blog posts, social media updates, and newsletters. Apfel handles the first draft. You polish and personalize.

Average time savings: 2-4 hours per week.

2. Email Management

Paste long email threads into Apfel. Ask for a summary. Get the key points in seconds.

Great for staying on top of client communication without reading every message.

3. Research Assistance

Feed Apfel industry reports or competitor analysis. Ask specific questions about the data.

Note: Apfel works with text you provide. It doesn't browse the internet for new information.

4. Proposal Writing

Input project requirements. Apfel creates proposal outlines. You add pricing and personal touches.

Cuts proposal writing time from 3 hours to 45 minutes.

5. Customer Support

Train Apfel on your FAQ responses. It suggests answers to common customer questions.

Faster response times improve customer satisfaction scores.

What This Actually Costs

Apfel itself is free. But there are hidden costs to consider.

Hardware Requirements

You need a Mac with Apple Silicon (M1, M2, or M3 chip). Older Intel Macs won't work well.

The app uses about 8GB of storage space. Make sure you have room.

Time Investment

Expect a learning curve. Budget 2-3 hours to explore features and find your workflow.

Most users see productivity gains after one week of daily use.

Compared to Alternatives

ChatGPT Plus costs $20 monthly. Claude Pro is $20 monthly. Over a year, that's $240-480 in subscription fees.

Apfel saves this cost while keeping your data private.

Limitations to Know About

Apfel isn't perfect. Here are the main drawbacks.

It only works on Mac computers. No Windows or web version exists.

The AI model is smaller than GPT-4. Responses are less sophisticated for complex tasks.

No internet access means no real-time information. You can't ask about current events or live data.

Integration with Your Current Tools

Apfel works best as part of a larger automation system. Here's how to connect it.

Use Hazel to automatically move documents to a folder. Then process them through Apfel for summaries.

Copy Apfel outputs into Zapier or Make workflows. This connects AI insights to your CRM or email tools.

Save common Apfel prompts as text snippets. Use TextExpander to insert them quickly.

Getting Started This Week

Download Apfel today. Test it with one regular task from your business.

Pick something you do weekly. Email drafting works well. So does document summarizing.

Track your time for one week. Note how much time Apfel saves versus doing the task manually.

Most solopreneurs find 5-8 hours of weekly savings. At typical consulting rates, that pays for a nice vacation by year-end.

The best AI tool is the one you'll actually use. Apfel's simplicity makes daily adoption easier than complex alternatives.
